### Part One: The Evolution of Human Civilization

#### [Chapter 1: The Fundamental Needs of Human Civilization](chapters/detailed/chapter01.html)
**Minimum cost, minimum constraints, maximum power, maximum freedom**

**Core Arguments:**
- What is "cost"? Material, time, freedom, dignity
- What are "constraints"? External coercion vs. internal limitations
- What is "power"? Choice, decision-making authority, influence
- What is "freedom"? Negative freedom vs. positive freedom

**Historical Evidence:**
- From tribes to empires, every civilizational transformation optimizes these four dimensions
- Why do some civilizations endure while others quickly perish?
- Trade-offs among the four dimensions: Impossible trinity? Or can all be achieved?

**Theoretical Framework:**
- Civilization assessment matrix
- Evolutionary dynamics
- Technological change and institutional lag

*Reading time: Approximately 35 minutes*

---

#### [Chapter 2: God's Civilization](chapters/detailed/chapter02.html)
**How to achieve (and truly realize) minimum cost, minimum constraints, maximum power, maximum freedom**

**Logic of Theocratic Civilization:**
- How divine absolute authority reduces coordination costs
- How faith internalizes constraints (no external coercion needed)
- How "God's will" legitimizes individual action

**Real Implementation:**
- Ancient Egypt, Mesopotamia, Mayan civilization cases
- Actual effects of religious codes (Code of Hammurabi, Mosaic Law)
- Power monopoly and corruption of priestly classes

**Why It Failed:**
- Struggle over interpretation of divine will
- Rise of scientific rationality
- Enormous cost of religious wars

**Legacy and Lessons:**
- Importance of spiritual support
- Cohesive power of common belief
- But cannot be built on unverifiable foundations

*Reading time: Approximately 40 minutes*
