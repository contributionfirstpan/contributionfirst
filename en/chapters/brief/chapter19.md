## Nature and Purpose of Law

### What Is Law?

**Definition:**
> Law is the formalized, enforceable system of social rules used to regulate behavior, resolve disputes, and maintain order.

**Three Elements:**
1. **Normative**: Clarifies what can and cannot be done
2. **Coercive**: Violations have consequences (punishment or restoration)
3. **Legitimate**: Considered just by society

---

    â†“

    â†“

```

**Purpose 3: Realize Justice**
```

    â†“

    â†“

**Purpose 4: Promote Change**

    â†“

```

## Fundamental Problems with Current Legal Systems

**Reality:**
```
Wealthy:
- Can afford best lawyers
- Can drag out lengthy litigation
- Know legal loopholes
    â†“
Poor:
- Cannot afford lawyers
- Cannot afford time
- Don't understand law
    â†“
Not equal before law
```

**Cases:**
- Large corporations violate â†’ Ordinary person sues â†’ Delay tactics â†’ Person gives up
- Wealthy commit crimes â†’ Luxury legal team â†’ Acquittal or light sentence
- Poor commit crimes â†’ Overburdened public defender â†’ Heavy sentence

#### Defect 2: Complex and Incomprehensible

**Legal Language:**
```

    â†“

**Consequences:**
- Must rely on lawyers (expensive)
- Easily deceived

#### Defect 3: Lag

**Law Updates Slowly:**
```

    â†“

    â†“

    â†“

- Internet, AI, gene editing â†’ Legal vacuum
- New types of crime â†’ No law to apply

#### Defect 4: Weak Enforcement

**Problems:**
```

    â†“

    â†“

    â†“

```

**Reasons:**
- Corruption (law enforcers bribed)
- Insufficient resources (not enough personnel)
- Power interference (protecting connections)
- Cumbersome procedures (low efficiency)

#### Defect 5: Punishment-Oriented

**Problem:**
```
Law mainly focuses on:
    â†“
Punishing wrongdoers
    â†“

- Preventing problems

- High recidivism

### Core of Rule of Law

**Not:**
> "Having laws is rule of law"

**But:**
> "Law is supreme, everyone equal, power limited"

**Four Pillars:**

**1. Law Supreme**
```
Anyone:
- Including president, wealthy, officials
- Must obey law
    â†“
No one "above law"
```

**2. Equal Protection**
```

- Regardless of wealth

```

**3. Power Limited**

**4. Procedural Justice**
```

- Process must also be fair
- Due process
- Cannot "end justifies means"
```
