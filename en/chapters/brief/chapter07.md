# Chapter 7: Ten Basic Principles
## The Constitutional Foundation of Contribution-First Civilization

---

## Why Do We Need "Basic Principles"?

### Principles vs. Rules

**Rules (Laws):**
- Specific, changeable
- "Don't run red lights"
- "8-hour workday"
- Can be modified based on circumstances

**Principles:**
- Fundamental, inviolable
- "Human dignity is inviolable"
- "All people are equal"
- Cannot be sacrificed for "efficiency" or "convenience"

---

### The "Constitution" of Contribution-First

**These ten principles are the foundation of Contribution-First Civilization:**
- No laws, policies, or decisions can violate them
- Like the U.S. Constitution's Bill of Rights
- Like the UN Universal Declaration of Human Rights

**But More Comprehensive:**
- Not just political rights
- Includes economic, social, psychological, ecological rights
- From "freedom from" (negative rights) to "access to" (positive rights)

---

## Principle 1: Basic Guarantee Principle

### Core Statement

> **All people, regardless of contribution level, receive basic care and protection.**

---

### Specific Content

#### 1. Material Guarantee (Basic Survival)

**Housing:**
- Everyone has safe, warm, private living space
- Not a mansion, but dignified
- Size standard: 25-30 mÂ² per person, increases with family size
- Location: With infrastructure (water, electricity, transport, community services)

**Food:**
- Nutritious, safe, diverse
- Not gourmet, but healthy and tasty
- Basic food free (cafeterias, food banks)
- Want better? Exchange with contribution value

**Clothing:**
- Seasonal, fitting, durable
- Not luxury brands, but decent
- Basic items free, fashionable items via contribution value

**Healthcare:**
- Comprehensive, free, high-quality
- Prevention, treatment, rehabilitation
- Won't go bankrupt or die from illness

**Education:**
- Kindergarten to university, lifelong learning
- Completely free
- Personalized, high-quality

---

#### 2. Emotional Guarantee (Psychological Care)

**When Lonely:**
- Community support networks
- AI emotional companionship (OmniHelp-type systems)
- Regular mental health check-ups

**In Crisis:**
- 24/7 psychological crisis hotline
- Free professional counseling
- Peer support groups

**During Growth:**
- Mentor systems
- Life coaches
- Continuous encouragement and guidance

---

#### 3. Safety Guarantee (Freedom from Violence and Fear)

**Physical Safety:**
- Freedom from violent attacks
- Safe public spaces
- Effective conflict mediation

**Economic Safety:**
- Won't starve from unemployment
- Won't be homeless from bankruptcy
- Basic guarantee never lost

**Future Safety:**
- No worry about dependent old age
- No worry about children's education
- No worry about accidental disasters

**Key Word: Regardless of contribution level**

**Even if you:**
- Completely don't work (but only basic guarantee)
- Made mistakes (after repair, still have guarantee)
- Are sick or disabled (even additional support)
- Are elderly and frail (dignified old age)

**Why?**

**1. Human Dignity Is Inviolable**
- Not valuable because "useful"

- Kant: "Humans are ends, not means"

**2. Avoid Fear-Driven Society**
- Current: Survival fear â†’ Forced to accept exploitation

**3. Unleash Human Potential**
- Maslow's hierarchy: Survival needs met â†’ Can pursue self-actualization

- Basic guarantee â†’ Creativity explosion

**4. Social Benefits**
- Reduce crime (desperation â†’ crime)
- Reduce disease (stress â†’ illness)
- Reduce social costs (prevention < governance)

## Principle 3: Needs Satisfaction Principle

### Core Statement

> **All people's basic needs are met, reasonable desires are supported.**

---

**Basic Needs (Unconditionally Met):**
1. Physiological: Food, water, sleep, health

4. Growth: Learning, development, capability enhancement

1. Better housing (villas, mansions)
2. Premium consumption (travel, gourmet food, luxury goods)
3. Special experiences (space travel, extreme sports)

**Unreasonable Desires (Not supported):**
1. Possessing others (slaves, control)
2. Harming others (violence, exploitation)
3. Destroying ecology (overconsumption, pollution)

## Principle 5: Free Choice Principle

### Core Statement

- Exploitation (forced labor)

- Systemic discrimination (institutional oppression)

- Severe environmental destruction
- Endangering future generations' survival
- Species extinction

**Except these, everything is free.**

## Principle 9: Restorative Justice Principle

### Core Statement

> **Conflicts and harms are resolved through repair, understanding, and growth, not punishment and revenge.**

---

### Specific Content

#### 1. Restorative Justice

**Not Asking:**
- Who's guilty?
- What punishment should they receive?

**But Asking:**
- Who was harmed?
- What's needed to repair?
- How to prevent recurrence?

**Process:**
```
Harm occurs
    â†“
Dialogue and understanding (victim, offender, community)
    â†“
Acknowledge harm and responsibility
    â†“
Repair plan (material, psychological, relational)
    â†“
Execute repair
    â†“
Reconciliation and reintegration
```

---

#### 2. Understanding Not Judgment

**Explore Roots:**
- Why did it happen?
- What factors led to it?
- How to change these factors?

**Humanize:**
- Offender isn't "demon"
- But person trapped in difficulties
- Needs help, not punishment

---

#### 3. Community Involvement

**Not Just Courts:**
- Victim, offender, community three parties
- Dialogue together
- Decide repair plan together

**Community Responsibility:**
- Not "lock them up and be done"
- But "how to help both parties"
- Prevent future conflicts

---

#### 4. Return to Society

**Not Permanent Label:**
- After repair, re-accept
- Not "ex-convict"
- But "person who once erred but has repaired"

**Support System:**
- Work, housing, community
- Don't abandon anyone

---

### Why Repair Not Punishment?

**1. Punishment Doesn't Solve Problems**
- Prison: Violence cycle (recidivism after release)
- Revenge: Endless retaliation
- Punishment makes people angrier, not better

**2. Repair Heals Harm**
- Victims need repair, not revenge
- Research: Restorative justice satisfaction > Traditional justice
- True closure

**3. Everyone Benefits**
- Offender grows (doesn't worsen)
- Victim heals (no hatred)
- Community reconciles (no division)

**4. Lower Cost**
- Prison: $30,000-80,000/person/year (USA)
- Repair: Lower cost, better effect

**Real Cases:**
- New Zealand: MÄori traditional "hui" (dialogue meetings)
- South Africa: Truth and Reconciliation Commission
- Result: Peaceful transition, no civil war

- Satisfy all principles simultaneously
