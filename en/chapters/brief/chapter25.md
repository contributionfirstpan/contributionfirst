## Core Buddhist Concepts

### Four Noble Truths

**1. Dukkha (Suffering)**
```
Life is suffering:
- Birth, aging, sickness, death
- Not getting what one wants
- Separation from loved ones
- Meeting with disliked ones
- Five aggregates aflame
    â†“
All is suffering
```

**Buddhist insight:**
- Suffering is fundamental reality of life
- Not pessimism, but honest facing

---

**2. Samudaya (Origin of Suffering)**
```
Cause of suffering:
- Craving (attachment, clinging)
- Aversion (anger, hatred)
- Ignorance (delusion, wrong view)
    â†“
Three poisons
```

**Buddhist insight:**
- Suffering originates from within
- Not external world (though it has influence)
- But our attachment to the world

---

**3. Nirodha (Cessation of Suffering)**
```
Suffering can cease:
- Extinguish craving, aversion, ignorance
    â†“
Nirvana:
- State without suffering
- Liberation, freedom, awakening
```

**Buddhist hope:**
- Suffering not fate
- Can be transcended

---

**4. Magga (Path to Cessation)**
```
Path to cessation:
- Noble Eightfold Path
    â†“
Right view, right intention, right speech,
right action, right livelihood,
right effort, right mindfulness, right concentration
    â†“
Through cultivation achieve awakening
```

## Comparing Buddhist Practice with Institutional Guarantee

**Method:**
